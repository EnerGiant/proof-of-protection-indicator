Consequence: Weighting By Energy With Fatality Potential

Enter the events for a period. A recordable count treats them all as equal. The severity weighting scales each event by the energy behind it, so a high-energy near miss carries the weight of many minor injuries. This severity-weighted account is the consequence measure. It rises with fatal energy, so to read it against assurance over time it is shown here on a scale where higher is safer, scaled against a fixed reference; in practice it is normalised against the organisation's own rolling baseline.

The Signal In The Gap

Assurance and consequence over twelve periods, both shown so that higher is safer. The value lives in the gap between them. Choose a scenario and watch what a rising assurance line means when the consequence line does not follow. An alert requires the gap to hold for three consecutive periods, because a single period is noise.
